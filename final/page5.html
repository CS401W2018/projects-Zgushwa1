Helpful Travel Tips for Visiting Mongolia

Heading to Mongolia? Here are a few things to keep in mind that can make your trip smoother and more enjoyable — especially if it's your first time.

  • Pack for different weather: The weather changes fast here. It might be warm during the day and chilly at night, even in summer. Bring layers just to be safe.
  • Be respectful around nomadic families: A lot of Mongolians still live a traditional, nomadic lifestyle. If you're visiting a ger (yurt), it's polite to accept food or drink when it's offered, even if it's just a small sip or bite.
  • Carry some cash: In the city, you can usually pay with a card. But once you're in the countryside, cash (in Mongolian Tugrik) is the way to go.
  • Don't count on strong Wi-Fi: You might not have internet access in rural areas. It helps to download maps and anything important before you head out.
  • Try the local food: Give dishes like buuz (steamed dumplings) or khuushuur (fried meat pies) a shot. Even if it's something new, it's part of the experience.
  • A local guide can really help: Especially outside the cities, a guide can help with language, directions, and understanding local customs.
  • Learn a few words: Even just saying “hello” (Sain bainuu) or “thank you” (Bayarlalaa) can go a long way and shows respect.

Mongolia is home to the world's largest equestrian statue, a massive 130-foot silver monument of Genghis Khan on horseback. You can actually go inside and take an elevator up to the horse's head for an amazing view.
